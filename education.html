Education-Outreach Program

Have You Ever Wondered What the Mountains of San Diego Have to Offer?

The Volcan Mountain Foundation's Education Program offers a variety of education venues designed to instill a lasting sense of wonder and responsibility for the natural world.

  • Interpretive guided hikes along our 1.5-mile Sky Island Trail, with views as far as the coast
  • Citizen science and stewardship projects contributing to real conservation research
  • Wildcrafting workshops exploring edible and medicinal plants of the oak forest
  • Nature studies for youth groups aligned with science standards for Grades 1–12 at the Keith and Priscilla Webb and Family Education Center
  • Customized outings for scout, family, service, and conservation organizations

Wildcrafting & Youth Programs

Wildcrafting Workshops & Videos

VMF's Wildcrafting series explores the edible and medicinal plants of Volcan Mountain's oak forests. Workshops are hands-on and grounded in Indigenous knowledge traditions of the Iipay people who have lived in this landscape for thousands of years.

The VMF Wildcrafting Video series brings these lessons to a wider audience — whether you can join us on the mountain or not.

Kids With Cameras

Youth Photography Program

VMF's Kids With Cameras program gives young photographers access to Volcan Mountain and teaches visual storytelling about conservation and the natural world. Student photographs have been exhibited publicly and used in VMF outreach.
