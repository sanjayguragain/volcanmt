Conservation & Stewardship

Preservation & Land Protection

Working with conservancy partners, VMF's vision is to acquire land for preservation from willing sellers and protect the entire 52,000-acre Volcan Mountain range.

🌲

Land Acquisition

We facilitate purchases of land from willing sellers to permanently protect habitat, water, and open space. Since 1988 we've conserved over 31,000 acres in the Volcan Mountain range.

🌿

Habitat Restoration

Managing invasive species, reintroducing native plants, reforesting burned habitat, and monitoring post-fire plant and animal recovery across the mountain range.

🏞️

Stewardship Planning

Developing and modeling sustainable land management practices — from non-native species control to watershed restoration and long-term ecological health.

Scientific Stewardship

Wildlife & Biodiversity Monitoring

Rigorous science guides our conservation decisions

🦅

Rare Bird Surveys

Annual raptor and songbird surveys track breeding populations and migration patterns across the mountain range, contributing to regional biodiversity databases.

🌺

Rare Plant Monitoring

Multiple sensitive plant species found only in San Diego County are monitored annually, with population data guiding habitat protection decisions.

🐾

Wildlife Corridors

Volcan Mountain serves as a critical linkage zone between the coast and inland desert. VMF monitors and protects wildlife movement corridors for mountain lion, mule deer, and other species.

Education & Outreach

Inspiring the Next Generation

VMF's Education-Outreach program brings environmental stewardship into classrooms and takes students to the mountain to experience the importance of habitat protection, watershed health, and natural history firsthand.

  • Classroom Programs — Curriculum-linked environmental education for K–12 students throughout San Diego County
  • Field Experiences — Guided hikes and hands-on restoration projects on Volcan Mountain
  • Sky Island Trail — Ancient oak, pine & cedar grove with Pipe Scope panoramic views
  • Nature Center Programs — Discovery-based learning at the Volcan Mountain Nature Center
  • Education Advisory Committee — Committed educators shaping our curriculum and partnerships

Art & Culture

Art & Volcan Mountain

Artistic expression has always played an important role in the Volcan Mountain Foundation. Just after VMF was incorporated in 1988, world-acclaimed artist and Julian local, James Hubbell, painted a watercolor of the mountain that was made into a poster to raise funds for the infant organization.

In 1990, with local volunteers, Hubbell built a beautiful gateway of native rock and cedar beams at the trailhead. His son Brennan built the tall iron sculptures in front of the gateway; Bill Porter added organic metalwork; Mirko Mrakajic carved the creatures on the wooden beams.

Joe Garcia, another internationally acclaimed Julian artist, donates posters and paintings to VMF's annual fundraiser and was the first honoree when VMF inaugurated the 'Artist of the Year' concept in 2010. San Diego photographer Blair Shamel has been documenting the seasons on Volcan since 2009.

Volcan Stories & Kids With Cameras

Photography, Film & Youth Visual Arts

VMF's Kids With Cameras program gives young photographers access to Volcan Mountain and teaches visual storytelling about conservation. Student photographs have been exhibited publicly and used in VMF outreach.

The Marjorie and Joseph Rubenson Endowment for Art and Science supports an annual artist residency on Volcan Mountain.
